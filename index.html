We saw Simone Craddock at the Ellington last night. I mean, I say "we" but it was just me who headed out there. Got there a bit early so headed across the street to the Leadlight for a drink and some food. The spicy chicken tenders were spicy, so I went for ice-cream after.
Getting in early and by myself is sort of a habit, so I ended up early and by myself inside. Waved to the waitress who I see there sometimes. She smiled on her way across, stopped and then came back.
She knew my name.
"Ronald."
"Darleigh."
Actually, is that how it's spelled? I hadn't really heard a name like that before, so I guessed either D-a-r-l-e-e or D-a-r-l-e-i-g-h.
Wrong. Dahli.
"You know the artist, Salvador Dali? It's based off of that."
"Well, my dad named me after Ronald Reagan."
She pulled such a wild expression.
"At least you can say it's a good story."

The person who sat at the table with me introduced herself as Shelley ? (I didn't ask for spelling clarification!) Possibly one of the most interesting people I have met. Fly-in fly-out health and safety officer for a minesite in the Northern Territory who had been spending the past eighteen months in her off-swings travelling around south-east Asia, who also went to the Ellington alone, has been living out of a duffle bag, does free-diving and is working on getting a motorcycle and her motorcycle licence, who grew up in a farm over east. She was in the hospitality industry for fifteen years running events and being sent to bartending championships before the pandemic threw a wrench it what seemed to be an untouchable industry, so she changed careers.
Just really fascinating and easy to talk to. Says she cares about and is interested in people, which I guess is a common factor between the two disciplines.
